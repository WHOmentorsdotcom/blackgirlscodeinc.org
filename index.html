BLACK GIRLS CODE INC.

was formed to advance equal opportunities within the continential United States of America (USA) for pre-collegiate scholars and executive trainees who may face prejudice and discrimination based on one, or more, of the legally protected cagetegories such as race, color, religion, sex (including pregnancy, sexual orientation, or gender identity), national origin, age (40 or older), disability and genetic information (including family medical history). National origin discrimination involves treating people (applicants or employees) unfavorably because they are from a particular country or part of the world, because of ethnicity or accent, or because they appear to be of a certain ethnic background (even if they are not).

As a nonprofit research organizsation, BLACK GIRLS CODE INC. studies employment conditions and opportunities in the community in order to eliminate discrimination in hiring.

By conducting discussions and lectures, BLACK GIRLS CODE INC. brings to the attention of partnering employers information about available skilled personnel, informs aspirants of means to improve their qualifications for wider occupational opportunities, and arranges interviews for qualified applicants with hiring managers from partnering employers.

BLACK GIRLS CODE INC. also works with the public schools in placing pre-collegiate scholars in actual career situations to improve or develop their specific capabilities.

Community Classroom is a term that refers to instructional methodology using on-the-job training experiences at business, industry, and public agency sites to assist students to acquire the competencies (skills, knowledge, and attitudes) necessary for entry-level employment.
